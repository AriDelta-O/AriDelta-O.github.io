Intro

This mech project was created as a hard surface modeling study focused on mechanical joints, clean shapes, and believable structure. I started with the legs and slowly moved upward, making sure each joint looked like it could actually function. This project helped me understand how mechanical parts support weight and motion.

Leg Assembly

The first stage covered the legs. I generated the wires with the GeoCables addon, which gave the legs a more grounded and industrial look without taking too much time. Keeping the shapes simple early on made it easier to refine the overall silhouette later.

Early leg work with GeoCables wiring.

Pelvis Blockout

After finishing the leg base I moved on to the pelvis. This part needed to connect the legs to the upper body in a way that felt stable. I experimented with different shapes before finding a design that supported the next stage without looking bulky.

Starting the pelvis and central joint layout.

Upper Body Progression

The upper body took the most time because it determined the entire silhouette of the mech. I developed it in small steps, adding new plates and adjusting proportions until the chest and core felt balanced. Each pass increased the detail while keeping the shapes readable.

Full clay model before adding final details.

Final Renders

The final renders show the mech fully assembled. I kept the materials simple so the focus stayed on the design and joint structure. The lighting setup highlights the shapes without hiding any of the mechanical elements.
