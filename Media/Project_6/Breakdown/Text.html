Intro

The idea for this project came from a fan art piece by Nyawame that I found on the Neuro-sama Discord server. The artwork showed Neuro inside a lava lamp. It was a fun idea, and I wanted to recreate it in Blender.

This is the original artwork that inspired the project.
Fan art by Nyawame.

Concept Board

Most of the references came from a single website. The base of the lamp was easy to model since it was mainly a circular form with simple bevels and clean edges.

Reference board for proportion and shapes.

Notes on Modeling

Getting the proportions right mattered more than the complexity. I imported one of the references as a plane and resized the model until it followed the silhouette closely.

Texturing was not a priority. I used simple cutouts from the references. Since the final scene depended on strong lighting and solid colors, detailed textures would have broken the look.

For the glass material, I set the IOR to 1.333. This value is close to water and helped reduce distortion so Neuro would stay visible inside the lamp.

The lava effect uses metaballs with a soft volumetric emission shader. I moved them slightly until the shapes floated in a natural way.

The red fading background is a single plane with a diffuse shader scaled behind the setup.

Initial Renders

These were early lighting tests. I played with the colors until the lamp, background and character blended well.

Early lighting adjustments.

Final Renders

The final scene includes the Neuro-sama logo and a simple base created in Canva. The lighting and colors were refined to give the lamp a strong glowing look.
