Intro

I have made several pieces of fan art based on the Vedalverse, but this one felt like a step up from everything I had done before. The idea came from the official music video Life. That video shows Neuro-sama as a humanoid robot with wires connected to her back. The visual style immediately caught my attention and I wanted to capture that feeling in my own way.

This is the frame that inspired the overall direction of the project.
Reference from the music video Life.

Concept Board

I started by planning a more complex setup that included different compartments for multiple versions of Neuro. Each one would have had its own HUD screens and GPU readouts. I eventually dropped this idea because it felt too crowded for the mood I wanted to create. The concept board helped me refine the focus toward a single presentation area with strong lighting and a cleaner background.

A simple concept board to shape the scene.

Initial Renders

The first renders came together faster than I expected. The hard surface models were simple enough to block out, but finding the right look took longer. I tried different layouts, different screen positions, and several color combinations before settling on something that matched the feeling of the original inspiration. I also had to rework the lighting so the background did not overpower Neuro.

Several early attempts before settling on the final direction.

Final Renders

The final version came out nicely after a lot of adjustments. I added HUD graphics near the end of the process. They were finished quickly, but they still helped bring more life to the scene. The background image is actually a screenshot of my own Arch Linux setup. It blended into the final shot better than I expected.

During post-processing I had to rethink the entire color grading because the scene leaned too far into blue tones. Adjusting the contrast and highlights made the metallic parts stand out more while keeping Neuro as the main focus.

Reference

The main inspiration was Neuro-sama's appearance in the music video Life. The rest of the design choices came from my own ideas and previous experience with mechanical characters.
